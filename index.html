Junior and Senior Design

Eligibility:
Within the Faculty of Engineering:
Junior: 1st and 2nd year students
Senior: 3rd year and above

Task:
Competitors must work within limited time and budget constraints to design, create, and test a physical prototype of their proposed solution.

Although it is recommended that teams are multidisciplinary, it is not necessary for success in the competition. However, it is recommended for the senior design competition as it may involve basic Arduino coding. Each team will be judged based on their design, application, teamwork, and performance, with regards to both their prototype and presentation.

The teams are made up of 4 students. Junior design is a 4 hour long competition and senior design is a 6 hour long competition. The problem is presented the day of the competition. Competitors must use only the material purchased in the Design Store.

Re-Engineering

Eligibility: This event is open to all undergraduate students in the Faculty of Engineering.

Task: Re-engineer a certain product based on the problem at hand

The Re-engineering team will be comprised of a maximum of two competitors. The case will be presented to the competitors on the competition start day. Each team will be required to write a report for the case as well as prepare a presentation to address the case in front of the judges. The competitors must address the social, environmental and economic impact of their solution.

Programming

Eligibility: This event is open to all undergraduate students in the Faculty of Engineering.

Task: Competitors must work in a limited time frame to plan, design and implement a industry-quality software, tackling the proposed problem

The challenge will put to test the team’s software development skills, project management and team working abilities, as well as technical documentation skills. The solution will be assessed by an industry judge panel. The winning solution will not necessarily be the most complex, but rather the most reasonable, with regards to the real world.
Teams of 4 students will be presented with a problem statement and program requirements. Teams will have six hours to develop a program which fits these criteria and have to prepare a 5 minute presentation to explain their solution to a panel of judges.

Consulting

Eligibility: This event is open to all undergraduate students in the Faculty of Engineering.

Task: As a team of 4, you will be given 6 hours to develop your solutions and produce a report exploring the details of your ideas. Additionally, your group will be presenting your solution to a panel of judges who will assess your effectiveness as engineering consultants.

Consulting is a category that addresses the tough questions that our society faces. As a consultant, you will be given a problem statement regarding one of major issues our world faces today.

Parliamentary Debate

Eligibility: This event is open to all undergraduate students in the Faculty of Engineering.

Task: Teams consisting of 2 students, will be given a series of debate topics and will compete in parliamentary style debates

Communications

Eligibility: This event is open to all undergraduate students in the Faculty of Engineering.

Task: Teams of either 1 or 2 students are to come prepared with their presentation (10-15 mins) in advance of the competition or attend the competition where they shall have 6 hours to prep.

MEC Communications gauges your ability to convert research, analysis and logic—on a technical topic of your choice—into a TED-styled talk. The catch? Your judges are laymen (not actually), who will not understand complex terminology. Why? Simply because the ultimate Communication Test as an Engineer is if you can explain your work and its significance in a technically-accurate, holistic and yet, intuitive way for society.
Victors will move on to Day 2, where a Champion will ultimately be selected to attend the Ontario Engineering Competition (OEC). Note that you are (A) allowed to create your presentation before the MEC Date; and (B) supposed to choose your own topic, which will be verified by the Organizing Committee (see guidelines). If you are preparing in advance, email your topic and a brief summary to Ben Li, Communication Coordinator at ⇒ lib83@mcmaster.ca

Innovation Design

This event is open to all undergraduate students in the Faculty of Engineering.
Capstone groups are encouraged to sign up and present their findings (how ever little or much it may be)

Task: Teams of 4 students are to come prepared with their presentation (10-15 mins) in advance of the second day of professional judging. Or they can attend the competition day where they have 6 hours to prep.

Competitors currently researching or interested in a cutting-edge engineering technology must present their findings to a panel of judges. These ideas are developed and researched prior to the competition. The professional level presentation must explore the findings related to the social, environmental and economic impacts of their technology.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

  • Who can Participitate?

    Everyone in the Faculty of Engineering can participate in the MEC! Check the Competition Categories page to see which categories you are eligible to compete in.

  • What is MEC?

    This event is a two-day competition, where over 300 engineering students are expected to compete across 8 categories. Teams of 4 (2 for debating, re-eng) work for 6 hours to build, test, prototype, and test their solution to a real-world problem. They are then judged by a panel of judges to determine the winners.

  • When is MEC?

    The MEC will run from October 20th 2018 to October 21st 2018 (contingent on your team making it to the second day), see Schedule for more details.

  • Why do I need to pay $10 to compete?

    The $10 fee for registration is a fully refunded deposit used to both secure your spot at the MEC and to help the organizers determine the quantity of material needed for dinner, breakfast, design store, T-shirts etc.

  • Do I even have a shot at winning at the CEC and OEC?

    Every year the challenges change and with that, the winner does also. 2 teams from McMaster made it all the way to CEC and one of them came in 2nd at CEC 2018!!!!!! McMasters own Debating team came 2nd at OEC, re-strategized and won 1st place at CEC2017!.

  • What does an all expenses paid trip to the OEC and CEC really mean?

    Last year the MES and Engineering Faculty were able to provide all 1st winners a spot at the OEC. This spot includes: admission to the OEC, all meals paid for and 2 nights stay at a hotel (OEC organizers choice). This package is valued at around $300-$400.

  • Do I need to have a group before I register or can I register for a category individually?

    You do not need to have a group to register, all participants register individually here. It is recommended to form a group prior to registration to ensure all group members secure a spot. It is also recommended to form a multidisciplinary team for the Junior and Senior design categories, although we understand most students do not have too many friends outside their stream. Students who come to compete without a group will be placed in a group with other group-less ccompetitors
